Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in neural computation and machine learning at Carnegie Mellon University. My research focuses on trying to understand how the brain learns and remembers motor movements, using concepts from statistics and machine learning. I work with Byron Yu, Steve Chase and Aaron Batista. I am also mentored by Rob Kass. My anticipated defense date is in the spring of 2023.

Previously, I worked at a start-up company where I used neural networks to classify EEG signals. I also completed my undergraduate in computer science and neurobiology at the University of Washington, where I researched under Rajesh Rao and Andrea Stocco on developing brain-computer interfaces.
